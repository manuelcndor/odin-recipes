Pollo a la brasa

Description

Pollo a la brasa, sometimes translated as “blackened chicken” or “charcoal chicken,” is Peru’s signature rotisserie dish characterized by its smoky aroma and golden, crisp skin. A whole chicken is rubbed with a secret marinade—commonly made from vinegar or lime juice, dark beer, soy sauce, garlic, cumin, ají panca paste, and fresh herbs—and left to marinate overnight. It’s traditionally roasted on a rotisserie over hardwood charcoal, which infuses the meat with a deep, savory flavor while keeping it juicy and tender.

Served alongside thick-cut French fries, a simple green salad, and often accompanied by creamy ají verde or other chili pepper–based sauces, pollo a la brasa is ubiquitous in Peru’s “pollerías” and celebrated nationwide. Every third Sunday of July, Peruvians commemorate Día del Pollo a la Brasa, underlining its status as the country’s most beloved dish, with up to 70% of diners naming it their favorite meal to eat out.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (about 1.5–2 kg)
  • ½ cup plain vinegar
  • ½ cup dark beer
  • ½ cup dark soy sauce
  • ½ cup ají panca paste
  • ½ cup garlic paste (or 4 cloves, minced)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary or thyme
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (for basting)

Steps

  1. Whisk vinegar, beer, soy sauce, ají panca, garlic, cumin, herbs, salt, and pepper to form a smooth marinade.
  2. Rub the mixture all over the chicken and refrigerate, covered, overnight.
  3. Preheat oven to 200 °C (390 °F). Place chicken on a rack over a roasting pan, roast for 90–120 min, basting occasionally with oil or pan juices, until skin is golden and crisp.
  4. Let the chicken rest 10 min, carve, and serve with fries, salad, and ají verde.
