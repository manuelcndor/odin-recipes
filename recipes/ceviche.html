Ceviche

Description

Ceviche is Peru’s most iconic dish, known for its fresh, zesty flavors and bold simplicity. It’s typically made from raw white fish—like sea bass or corvina—marinated in freshly squeezed lime juice, which “cooks” the fish by denaturing the proteins. The fish is mixed with sliced red onions, a touch of chili pepper (often ají limo), and seasoned with salt and cilantro.

It’s traditionally served with sides like sweet potato and Peruvian corn (choclo), whose softness and starch balance the tangy punch of the citrus marinade. Ceviche is a symbol of Peruvian culinary pride, celebrated for its harmony of textures and regional variations. It’s best enjoyed freshly prepared, often as a midday meal, especially along the coast where the catch is at its freshest.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg white fish fillet (sea bass, mahi-mahi), cut into 2 cm cubes
  • Juice of ½ kg limes and ½ kg lemons (about 1 cup)
  • 1 small purple onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 habanero or ají limo peppers, seeded and chopped
  • ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tsp grated ginger (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • For serving: boiled sweet potato slices, cooked yellow corn, plantain chips

Steps

  1. Place fish cubes in a bowl, pour over citrus juice, stir gently, and refrigerate 20–30 min until opaque.
  2. While fish “cooks,” steam sweet potatoes, fry plantain chips, and soak onion in salted water then drain.
  3. Drain most of the marinade, add onion, peppers, cilantro, ginger, and salt; toss lightly and chill 5–10 min.
  4. Plate ceviche with sweet potato, corn, and plantain chips; serve immediately.
